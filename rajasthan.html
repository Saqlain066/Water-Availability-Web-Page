RAJASTHAN

Rajasthan has one of the lowest per capita renewable water resources in the
world with an average annual per capita renewable freshwater availability of
532 cubic meters which is only slightly more than the international minimum of
500 cubic meters needed to avert what is referred to as a condition of absolute water .

Unfortunately Rajasthan is the driest state in the country and is water scarce (having per capita water availability below 1000 m3/year) since 1991.


Water Recive

Rajasthan received 9.051 million acre-foot (MAF) Ravi Beas water this year,
which is highest in last five years

Main Source of Water






Chambal is the principal river for water supply in the state of Rajasthan. It originates at Janapav
which is south of Mhow town, on the south slope of Vindhyan range.








Causes of Water Shortage

Rajasthan, located in the arid region of India, often faces water scarcity due
to its low rainfall and limited water resources. To cope with water shortages,
various measures are taken: Rainwater Harvesting: Capturing and storing rainwater
during the monsoon season for later use is a common practice in Rajasthan.

Solution of Water Shortage






Rainwater Harvesting: Capturing and storing rainwater during the monsoon season for later use
is a common practice in Rajasthan. This helps recharge groundwater and provides a supplemental water source during dry periods.
