MAHARASHTRA

The State is mainly covered by the basins of Krishna, Godavari and Tapi
except the west-flowing rivers of Konkan strip. A small portion on north comes under
Narmada basin. The major rivers, which flow through Maharashtra state are Godavari, Krishna and Tapi.


Water Recive

The total available of water is 195968 Mcum in Maharashtra state. Out of these
163820 Mcum is surface water and 32148 Mcum is ground water.



Main Source Of Water





The Godavari (IAST: Godavari) is India's second longest river after the Ganga river and
drains into the third largest basin in India, covering about 10% of India's total geographical area.
Its source is in Trimbakeshwar, Nashik, Maharashtra.







Causes of Water Shortage

Over-exploitation of ground water in Maharashtra has resulted in the depletion of water
from dams and reservoirs.
